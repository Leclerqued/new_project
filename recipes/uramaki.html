Uramaki

Uramaki (裏巻, "inside-out roll") is a medium-sized cylindrical style of sushi with two or more fillings and was developed as a result of the creation of the California roll, as a method originally meant to hide the nori.

Uramaki differs from other makimono because the rice is on the outside and the nori inside.

The filling is surrounded by nori, then a layer of rice, and optionally an outer coating of some other ingredients such as roe or toasted sesame seeds.

It can be made with different fillings, such as tuna, crab meat, avocado, mayonnaise, cucumber, or carrots.

Unagi Uramaki

Unagi Sushi Rolls are filled with bbq eel, crunchy cucumber, then rolled in nori seaweed sheet and sushi rice; it is usually drizzled with unagi sauce for extra flavor.

Ingredients
  • Unagi
  • Cucumber/Avocado
  • Sushi Rice
  • Sushi Vinegar
  • Nori
  • Unagi Sauce
  • Sesame seeds
Preparation
  1. Cook sushi rice
  2. Prepare unagi
  3. Make the unagi roll
